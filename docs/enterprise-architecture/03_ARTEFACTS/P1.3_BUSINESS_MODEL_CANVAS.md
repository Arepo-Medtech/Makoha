# Makoha Business Model Canvas — Regulated Pharmacy Consultation Assurance

**Version:** 1.1  
**Date:** 21 July 2026  
**Status:** PROVISIONAL — CUSTOMER, COST AND CLINICAL-SERVICE VALIDATION REQUIRED

| Canvas element | Makoha working thesis |
|---|---|
| Customer segments | First: regional and multi-site Australian pharmacy groups operating or preparing for expanded-scope pharmacist services. Later: larger banners, health systems, insurers and clinical-system/platform vendors. |
| Economic buyer | Group owner/executive, operations leader or clinical-governance lead. Site owners join through authorised participation instruments where group authority is insufficient. |
| Users, beneficiaries and payers | Authorised pharmacist is the accountable professional user. Patients are beneficiaries and may pay a disclosed per-consult fee. Pharmacy groups, sites, employers, insurers, health systems or other contracted payers may subsidise access and doctor capacity. |
| Value proposition | A regulated, human-in-the-loop assurance layer that makes expanded-scope consultations more consistent, traceable and audit-ready while preserving pharmacist judgement, escalation and complete records. |
| Customer problem | Scaling services across sites multiplies variation in exclusions, red flags, documentation, escalation, communication and governance evidence. |
| Alternatives | Existing clinical/dispensing systems, internal checklists and audits, Qiri-style pharmacy clinical reasoning, documentation tools such as Heidi/Lyrebird, or manual clinical supervision. |
| Channels | Founder-led design partnerships; clinical-governance and operations buyers; later clinical-system integrations, professional partnerships and enterprise procurement. |
| Customer relationships | High-trust, governed onboarding; named executive and clinical sponsors; service reviews; change control; safety monitoring; renewals based on evidence rather than lock-in. All contracts non-exclusive. |
| Revenue | Fixed-fee evidence projects; annual enterprise base licence; site and optional clinician/user bands; transparent patient per-consult fees; B2B/payer per-consult or minimum-capacity service agreements funding/subsidising access and doctor availability; one-time implementation/integration; later API/platform or field-of-use licensing. |
| Excluded revenue | Prescription revenue share, prescription-contingent charges, clinical outcome fees, sale of patient data, surprise billing and exclusivity payments. |
| Key activities | Product and integration engineering; clinical safety and evidence; QMS and regulatory maintenance; security/privacy; implementation; monitoring; customer success; controlled product change. |
| Key resources | Regulated workflow architecture; safety case and design history; clinical and quality authority; permissioned evidence; integration adapters; audit/provenance capability; brand and partner trust. |
| Key partners | Design-partner pharmacy groups and authorised sites; contracted doctor/telehealth providers; regulatory/quality counsel; clinical-governance authorities; ethics/evidence partners; clinical-system vendors; hosting/model suppliers; payers; cyber, legal and indemnity providers. |
| Cost structure | Doctor availability and delivered consults; engineering; cloud/model use; security; clinical governance; quality/regulatory work; evidence generation; integrations; support; payment processing; waivers/refunds; insurance; legal/compliance and enterprise sales. |
| Defensibility | Permissioned longitudinal assurance events + verified safety/effectiveness evidence + regulated operating system + workflow integration. Not model weights or raw data ownership. |
| Core metrics | ARR and gross margin by group/site; implementation time and reuse; sales cycle; activation and retained use; support effort; record completeness; escalation quality; critical safety events; audit findings; renewal/expansion; data-rights coverage. |

## Clinical and consumer guardrails

- Clinical suitability, escalation and prescribing decisions are independent of revenue.
- Urgent or emergency referral is never delayed for payment or billing administration.
- Fees, inclusions, likely doctor charges and reasonable alternatives are disclosed before consent wherever clinically practicable.
- Subsidy, waiver, hardship and refund rules are defined before launch.
- Doctor availability is secured through service levels and minimum funding, not assumed from incidental referral volume.
