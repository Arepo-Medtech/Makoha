Move Makoha from an advanced synthetic/MVP build toward an enterprise, regulated evidence position without treating calendar progress as authority for patient activity.

## Workstream outcomes

### Regulatory and quality

- Qualified classification memorandum.
- Intended-purpose and product-boundary control.
- Operational QMS covering design, risk, software, usability, evidence, cyber, CAPA and post-market planning.
- Inspection-ready design-history/evidence structure appropriate to stage.

### Clinical authority and service model

- Appointed medical, pharmacist, clinical-safety and quality/regulatory authorities.
- Active Clinical Safety and AI Governance Committee.
- Doctor-capacity, handover, provider-of-record and billing structure separately approved.
- Clinical decisions and referrals audited as independent of revenue.

### Product and evidence

- Fresh build/gate evidence and reconciled release blockers.
- Narrow evidence question linked to the first commercial wedge.
- Synthetic verification before any patient-data activity.
- Human-factors, privacy, security, protocol and authority readiness before live evidence.

### Commercial

- Pharmacy 777 discovery completed without implying site authority or exclusivity.
- Existing-system duplication tested before build expansion.
- Paid evidence phase separately negotiated.
- Pricing and COGS tested; no production revenue assumed in the 12-month budget.
- At least one comparable second-account path to reduce single-partner dependence.

## Cadence

- Weekly: runway/cash, critical risks, partner actions and blocking evidence.
- Fortnightly: product/quality/clinical change-control review.
- Monthly: budget reforecast, register reconciliation and gate review.
- Quarterly: founder/board continue, narrow, pivot or stop decision.
- Event-driven: immediate review after a critical incident, material model/source change, classification change, data breach or partner authority change.

## Non-negotiable sequencing

1. Finance truth before committing the annual plan.
2. Intended purpose and authority before clinical claims.
3. QMS/design controls before regulated expansion.
4. Synthetic evidence before live evidence.
5. Full G4 readiness before patient data or clinical reliance.
6. Signed evidence conclusions before performance claims.
7. Verified runway before the next expansion commitment.

## Completion definition

The plan succeeds if Makoha reaches July 2027 with a controlled regulatory and authority posture, enterprise QMS core, credible design-partner evidence, verified economics and sufficient runway to make a deliberate next decision. Shipping a patient-facing function is not itself a success criterion.
