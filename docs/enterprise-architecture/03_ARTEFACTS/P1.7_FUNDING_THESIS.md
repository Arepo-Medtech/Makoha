# Makoha Funding Thesis — Enterprise Evidence Stage

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 21 July 2026  
**Status:** PROVISIONAL — FINANCE, CAP-TABLE AND ADVISER VERIFICATION REQUIRED

## Thesis

Makoha should raise against **risk retirement and enterprise evidence**, not against an unqualified “AI doctor” growth story.

The investable sequence is:

1. confirm regulated product boundaries and authority;
2. install a Class III-capable operating and quality system;
3. demonstrate governed, non-duplicative value with a pharmacy design partner;
4. prove deployment economics and lawful data/evidence rights; and
5. use those assets to support a larger clinical, regulatory and commercial round.

The selected Base enterprise evidence scenario requires **[ASSUMPTION — LOW CONFIDENCE] $1,475,440 before verified opening cash and liabilities**, comprising:

- $1,271,200 gross 12-month spend;
- less $50,000 potential governed evidence revenue; and
- plus $254,240 minimum reserve.

## Capital story

### Problem being financed

Expanded-scope pharmacist care creates an assurance and continuity problem across clinical scope, documentation, escalation and doctor access. Generic documentation tools do not resolve the regulated evidence and cross-site governance burden.

### Asset being built

Makoha is building permissioned longitudinal consultation-assurance evidence, a regulated operating system, reusable integrations and a pharmacist-to-doctor continuity layer.

### Milestones purchased by capital

- Qualified regulatory path and controlled intended purposes.
- Clinical, pharmacy, safety and quality authority.
- Enterprise QMS and design history.
- Controlled product/integration assurance.
- Governed design-partner discovery and evidence readiness.
- Pricing, COGS and second-account evidence.

### What capital does not buy automatically

- TGA/ARTG inclusion.
- Clinical validation.
- Patient-facing release.
- Pharmacy 777 commitment or rollout.
- Production or patient-fee revenue.
- Ownership of patient data.

## Staging

### Control-stage bridge — only if required by verified runway

Fund the first control block: finance truth, counsel, authority recruitment, QMS assessment and partner discovery. The amount is calculated from the monthly model and actual opening cash; it is not pre-approved here.

### Principal pre-seed

Fund the Base enterprise evidence plan with sufficient reserve to avoid safety or quality compromises caused by cash pressure. A single adequately sized close is preferable to continuous fundraising if achievable without unacceptable dilution or control terms.

### Next institutional round

Begin only after Makoha can show signed regulatory/quality evidence, credible authority, governed partner evidence, repeatable deployment economics and a non-exclusive multi-account pipeline.

## Founder-control and strategic-rights policy

- No partner or investor exclusivity.
- No transfer of background IP or patient/customer data ownership.
- No broad field restriction, price parity or right of first refusal over a company sale.
- No investor veto over clinical decisions, incidents, regulatory reporting or safety action.
- Dilution model must include the current register, instrument conversion, any option pool and the next round.
- Strategic commercial rights must be separately valued, time-limited and revocable for non-performance.
- Funding documents require Australian corporate, tax and securities advice.

## Investor fit

Prioritise investors able to understand regulated enterprise health technology, long evidence cycles and non-exclusive health-system/pharmacy partnerships. Avoid investors whose return thesis depends on rapid patient deployment, unchecked prescribing volume, data ownership or partner lock-in.

## Kill criteria

- Financing terms materially impair founder control, IP or non-exclusive market access.
- The raise cannot fund the quality, clinical and regulatory functions needed for the proposed scope.
- Investors require patient deployment before the release and evidence gates.
- The business must rely on uncontracted revenue or grants to remain solvent during safety-critical work.
- Pharmacy system duplication or customer evidence invalidates the wedge.

## Required founder decisions before launch

- Verified cash, liabilities, burn and minimum personal compensation.
- Target raise timing and maximum acceptable dilution.
- Instrument preference after legal/tax advice.
- Investor inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- Option-pool strategy and hiring model.
- Minimum reserve and downside stop point.
