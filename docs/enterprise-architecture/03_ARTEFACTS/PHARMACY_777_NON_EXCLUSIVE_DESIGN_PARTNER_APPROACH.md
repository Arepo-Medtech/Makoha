# Pharmacy 777 — Non-Exclusive Design-Partner Approach

**Status:** FOUNDER-DIRECTED — PROCEED; TOOWOOMBA COUNTERPARTY TBC  
**Date:** 20 July 2026  
**Decision:** M-D011  
**Commercial principle:** every discussion, evaluation and contract is non-exclusive

## Decision statement

Pharmacy 777 is Makoha's preferred first design-partner candidate. The proposed sequence is to contract through the Western Australian leadership structure, use the existing Queensland footprint to study an operating expanded-scope prescribing workflow, and expand into Western Australia only after evidence, regulatory and quality gates permit.

This is a target decision, not evidence of Pharmacy 777 interest, consent, commitment or partnership.

**Founder instruction:** proceed without treating the exact Toowoomba ownership structure as a discovery blocker. Record it as `TBC`. A split group/franchisor/local-owner counterparty structure is a low-confidence assumption, not a fact.

## Why Pharmacy 777 is the preferred candidate

1. **Western Australian origin and leadership proximity.** Pharmacy 777 states that its history began in Applecross in 1962. Founder-provided information places key personnel in Perth.
2. **Material but tractable network.** The current Pharmacy 777 website reports 87 branded Australian locations, while a current external governance biography describes a wider group network of more than 100 stores. This is large enough to create cross-site governance value while permitting a deliberately small pilot subset; the definition of the wider network must be confirmed.
3. **Queensland development bridge.** Pharmacy 777 lists a Toowoomba location and currently advertises prescribing-pharmacist consultations at that location. This provides a credible current-workflow discovery site before WA's broader EACPP expansion.
4. **Demonstrated digital-health adoption.** Pharmacy 777 has published material supporting telehealth, electronic prescriptions, My Health Record and regional digital-health access, and currently offers a telehealth-to-eScript-to-delivery pathway.
5. **Clinical-services fit.** The group positions itself around pharmacist-delivered health services, personalised care and group-wide practice support—closely aligned to Makoha's governance-first consultation-assurance wedge.
6. **WA expansion logic.** A successful, lawfully governed Queensland evidence phase could inform a controlled WA deployment pattern without requiring a different counterparty.

2. A non-exclusive participation schedule for each pharmacy premises, authorised by the relevant owner/operator.

4. A site/group data-processing and security schedule.

## Proposed development sequence

### Stage 0 — Authority and fit diligence

- Resolve whether Pharmacy 777 (Aust) Pty Ltd, 777 Franchising Pty Ltd, a site owner or another group entity is the contracting counterparty, and confirm group authority versus independent/franchise pharmacy owners.
- Map the clinical-governance, technology, privacy, franchise/member-support and procurement owners.
- Confirm the Toowoomba service scope, approved clinical information system and pharmacist authorisations.
- Identify existing AI/CDSS, workflow, documentation and integration vendors.
- Test whether Makoha complements or duplicates the current system.

**Exit:** an authorised Pharmacy 777 contact agrees to a non-product discovery process and provides a current-workflow owner. Exact Toowoomba ownership may remain `TBC` at this point if no contract, data access or site activity occurs.

### Stage 1 — Non-clinical co-design

- Run structured buyer, pharmacist and technology interviews.
- Map the current consultation, exclusion, escalation, referral, documentation and audit workflow.
- Agree the problem statement and success/failure measures.
- Use demonstrations based only on synthetic data; make no clinical-use or regulatory-approval claim.

**Exit:** a written, non-exclusive evaluation intent with approved scope and evidence plan. The intent must identify Toowoomba counterparty confirmation as a pre-execution condition.

### Stage 2 — Queensland evidence phase

- Prefer the Toowoomba location because it is the currently verified Pharmacy 777 Queensland site offering prescribing-pharmacist consultations.
- Before execution or site activity, confirm the local owner/operator, pharmacy-business authority, group/franchise permissions, employing entity, data roles and authorised signatories.
- Begin with synthetic, retrospective de-identified or other counsel-approved shadow evaluation.
- Do not allow Makoha output to determine patient care.
- Measure record completeness, eligibility/exclusion capture, escalation, communication, pharmacist time, usability and existing-system overlap.

**Exit:** prespecified safety, usefulness and non-duplication thresholds pass; required regulatory, ethics, privacy, security and contract approvals are recorded.

### Stage 3 — Controlled Western Australian expansion

- Select a small Perth and regional site cohort with trained/authorised pharmacists.
- Re-run site readiness, integration, human-factors and workflow checks.
- Expand by evidence gate, not by calendar or store count.
- No patient-facing or clinical deployment until P1.6 regulatory and release gates are satisfied.

**Exit:** repeatable implementation evidence across at least two distinct WA workflow settings.

### Stage 4 — Broader network option

Any broader rollout requires a new investment decision, updated regulatory change-impact review and a separate non-exclusive commercial order or schedule.

## Non-exclusive contracting principles

These principles require Australian legal and regulatory review before execution:

- No exclusivity by geography, pharmacy segment, clinical condition, product function, channel or time period.
- Pharmacy 777 remains free to build, buy or use competing products.
- Makoha remains free to work with other pharmacy groups, clinicians and health-system partners.
- No right of first refusal, right of first negotiation or veto over Makoha product development, fundraising, partnering or market entry.
- No equity, pharmacy ownership interest, loan, rebate, profit share or outcome-linked control arrangement.
- Prefer a clear arm's-length evaluation agreement and, if validated, a separately priced software/service agreement.
- Each party retains its background intellectual property. Pharmacy 777-specific confidential materials remain protected; Makoha retains its platform and generalisable improvements, subject to agreed data and confidentiality boundaries.
- Pharmacy/patient data may be used only for the approved purpose. No secondary model training or reuse of identifiable information without specific lawful authority.
- Pharmacists retain clinical judgement and professional accountability. Makoha must not be framed as autonomous diagnosis or prescribing.
- Publicity, logo use, testimonials and case studies require separate written approval.
- Either party may stop the evaluation for safety, regulatory, privacy, security or evidence-threshold failure without creating commercial lock-in.

## Draft initial approach

**Subject:** Non-exclusive consultation-assurance research with Pharmacy 777

Makoha is developing a governance-first consultation-assurance platform for expanded-scope pharmacist services. It is intended to support authorised pharmacists with protocol-aligned documentation, exclusions, escalation and audit evidence while preserving the pharmacist's clinical judgement and accountability.

Pharmacy 777's Western Australian leadership, current prescribing-pharmacist service in Toowoomba and demonstrated adoption of digital-health pathways make the group a strong potential design partner. We would value a 45-minute discovery discussion with the appropriate clinical-governance and technology leaders to understand the current workflow and determine whether a tightly governed, non-exclusive evaluation would add value alongside existing systems.

This is not a request for live clinical deployment. Any evaluation would begin with synthetic, retrospective de-identified or otherwise approved evidence methods and would remain subject to regulatory, privacy, security and clinical-governance approval.
