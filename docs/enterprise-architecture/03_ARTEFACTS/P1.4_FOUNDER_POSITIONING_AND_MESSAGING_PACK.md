## Master positioning

### Company

For pharmacy groups expanding pharmacist-led care, Makoha is a regulated consultation-assurance and continuity platform that helps pharmacists work consistently within scope and connects patients to doctor care when a presentation exceeds that scope. Unlike generic scribes or isolated decision tools, Makoha is designed around traceable evidence, longitudinal continuity and accountable human judgement.

### Founder

Dr Ken Lee is the clinician-founder of Makoha, building an Australian health-technology platform around a simple principle: AI should make accountable clinical judgement easier to exercise and easier to evidence—never replace it.

### Category line

**Accountable continuity infrastructure for pharmacist-led care.**

## Ready-to-use biographies

### One-line biography

Dr Ken Lee is the clinician-founder of Makoha, an Australian health-technology venture developing accountable consultation-assurance and care-continuity infrastructure for pharmacist-led care.

### Short biography

Dr Ken Lee is the clinician-founder and director behind Makoha. He is developing the platform to help pharmacists work safely within defined scope, preserve accountable human judgement and connect patients to doctor or urgent care when a presentation needs more. Makoha remains in controlled development and is planning for a regulated medical-software pathway.

### Partner introduction

Dr Ken Lee founded Makoha to help pharmacy groups expand pharmacist-led care without losing consistency, traceability or continuity. The platform is being designed as a regulated assurance layer: it supports the pharmacist’s assessment and record, preserves professional accountability and enables an appropriate doctor or urgent-care pathway beyond pharmacist scope. Makoha is currently seeking governed, non-exclusive design partnerships; no patient-ready or regulator-approved claim is made.

### Longer profile

Dr Ken Lee is the clinician-founder of Makoha and director of Arepo Medtech Pty Ltd. His work on Makoha focuses on a recurring healthcare problem: clinicians and patients often move between services without a sufficiently complete, traceable picture of what has already been considered and decided.

Makoha’s first application is expanded-scope pharmacist care. The system is intended to help pharmacists capture relevant information, recognise exclusions and red flags, complete an auditable record and preserve continuity when doctor or urgent care is required. The architecture is explicitly human-in-the-loop: registered professionals remain responsible for clinical decisions.

Makoha is in controlled development using synthetic data. Regulatory classification, clinical evidence and patient-facing release remain gated. Ken’s role is founder, product visionary and clinical problem-framer; formal medical, pharmacy, regulatory, quality and safety authorities will govern their respective reserved matters.

## Audience-specific messages

| Audience | Lead message |
|---|---|
| Pharmacy executive | Expand pharmacist-led care with evidence that scope, escalation and documentation remain controlled across sites. |
| Pharmacist | Makoha supports your judgement and helps evidence it; it does not make the decision for you. |
| Doctor/provider network | Receive structured, traceable handovers when a case exceeds pharmacist scope, under contracted clinical and service standards. |
| Patient | Start with accessible pharmacist-led care where appropriate, with a clear path to a doctor or urgent care when it is not. |
| Regulator/quality adviser | Intended-purpose discipline, separable product boundaries, traceable evidence and controlled human authority are designed in from the beginning. |
| Investor | The commercial opportunity is an assurance and continuity layer across pharmacy and medical care, with recurring enterprise, consult and platform revenue; all clinical and economic claims remain evidence-gated. |
