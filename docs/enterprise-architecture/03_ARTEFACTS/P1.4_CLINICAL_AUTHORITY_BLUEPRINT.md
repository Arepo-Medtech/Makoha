# Makoha Clinical-Authority Blueprint

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 21 July 2026  
**Status:** PROPOSED — APPOINTMENTS AND TERMS OF REFERENCE REQUIRED

## Operating principle

Makoha is founder-led but cannot be clinically self-certified. Authority follows verified competence, role, jurisdiction and product boundary. Patient-facing release and material safety decisions require documented multi-role approval.

## Clinical Safety and AI Governance Committee

### Minimum early-phase voting members

- Founder/accountable executive.
- Medical director.
- Pharmacist clinical lead.
- Clinical safety officer.
- Quality/regulatory lead.
- Consumer representative for patient-facing or patient-fee matters.

Privacy/security and evidence advisers attend whenever their reserved matter is considered. Committee independence should increase with clinical exposure and scale.

### Reserved matters

The founder cannot approve these alone:

1. intended purpose or classification change;
2. activation of a clinical condition, population or jurisdiction;
3. clinical protocol, exclusion, red-flag or escalation change;
4. patient-data or patient-facing evidence phase;
5. production/patient eligibility release;
6. model, source or integration change with material clinical impact;
7. acceptance of a critical/residual safety risk;
8. serious incident, field action or regulator notification decision;
9. clinical evidence conclusion or safety/effectiveness public claim; and
10. patient-fee/referral design that may affect access or clinical incentives.

### Provisional quorum

**[ASSUMPTION — TO BE LEGALLY AND OPERATIONALLY REVIEWED]** A reserved-matter decision requires the founder/accountable executive or delegate, the directly relevant medical or pharmacist lead, the clinical safety officer and the quality/regulatory lead. A person with a material conflict cannot vote. Patient-facing fee/access decisions also require consumer review.

## Release authority model

```text
Product/engineering evidence complete
              ↓
Clinical safety recommendation
              ↓
Medical or pharmacist protocol-owner approval
              ↓
Quality/regulatory release confirmation
              ↓
Executive operational authorisation
              ↓
Patient eligibility remains fail-closed unless every approval is current
```

No single signature substitutes for the full sequence.

## Appointment pack required for each authority

- Role description and decision rights.
- CV and verified qualifications.
- Registration/licence and scope check where applicable.
- Professional indemnity and insurance relevance.
- Conflict-of-interest declaration.
- Confidentiality, IP and data obligations.
- Time commitment, remuneration and term.
- Delegation and absence coverage.
- Performance, training and continuing-competence requirements.
- Removal, incident and whistleblowing provisions.
- Permission for any public biography or endorsement.

## External authority-page rule

Publish only appointed people who consent. For each, state the actual role and remit, not a broad “adviser” label. Do not imply endorsement by a former employer, professional body, university or regulator.

## Gate

This blueprint does not create authority. R-M019 remains open until the required roles are appointed, credentials verified, conflicts recorded, terms of reference approved and reserved-matter workflow tested.
