## Strongest trust objection

One clinician-founder cannot credibly be the sole source of product vision, clinical evidence, pharmacist governance, doctor-network oversight, regulatory classification, quality release and safety adjudication—particularly under a Class III-capable assurance target.

The answer is not to minimise founder authority. It is to define it precisely and surround it with verifiable, profession-specific and independent authority.

## Positioning decision

Makoha will be positioned as **founder-led, clinician-founded and independently governed**.

Ken owns:

- mission, values and problem framing;
- product and system vision;
- founder-level clinical perspective;
- enterprise and partnership narrative; and
- executive accountability for appointing appropriately qualified authorities.

Ken does not claim unilateral authority for:

- regulatory classification or conformity;
- pharmacy scope/protocol approval;
- medical service delivery or doctor credentialing;
- clinical evaluation conclusions;
- patient-facing release;
- serious-incident adjudication; or
- quality-system certification.

Those decisions require the accountable roles and reserved-matter process in `03_ARTEFACTS/P1.4_CLINICAL_AUTHORITY_BLUEPRINT.md`.

## Core positioning

### Enterprise positioning statement

> For pharmacy groups expanding pharmacist-led care, Makoha is a regulated consultation-assurance and continuity platform that helps pharmacists work consistently within scope and connects patients to doctor care when a presentation exceeds that scope. Unlike generic scribes or isolated decision tools, Makoha is designed around traceable evidence, longitudinal continuity and accountable human judgement.

### Founder positioning statement

> Dr Ken Lee is the clinician-founder of Makoha, building an Australian health-technology platform around a simple principle: AI should make accountable clinical judgement easier to exercise and easier to evidence—never replace it.

### Category line

**Accountable continuity infrastructure for pharmacist-led care.**

This category line is preferred to “AI doctor” in enterprise, regulatory and clinical-governance communications. “AI doctor” may be used only as a clearly qualified vision or concept and never as a claim that software is a registered practitioner, autonomously diagnoses, prescribes or provides care.

## Founder narrative

### Publishable evidence-safe narrative

Dr Ken Lee founded Makoha to address a recurring problem in healthcare: important decisions are often made with incomplete context, under time pressure, across disconnected services. Makoha is being developed so pharmacists and doctors can work from a clearer, traceable clinical picture while keeping responsibility with the registered practitioner.

The first application is pharmacist-led care. Pharmacists can manage defined presentations within scope, but safe access also depends on recognising exclusions and red flags, documenting decisions and connecting patients to doctor or urgent care when the case falls outside that scope. Makoha is designed as the assurance and continuity layer across that boundary.

The company’s position is deliberately restrained: the current system remains in controlled development, regulatory and clinical-authority gates remain open, and no patient-ready or regulator-approved claim is made. The ambition is enterprise-grade clinical infrastructure whose evidence is as important as its intelligence.

### Story expansion held pending evidence

The legacy kickoff refers to a “night shifts” origin narrative, but the exact event, setting and lesson are absent from the controlled source set. It must not be reconstructed from inference. Once Ken supplies the facts, the story should connect:

1. the real clinical situation and pressure;
2. what fragmented information or workflow made difficult;
3. why a human professional still had to own the decision;
4. the insight that led to Makoha’s assurance architecture; and
5. the benefit to pharmacists, doctors and patients.

## Objection pre-emption

| Objection | Evidence-safe response |
|---|---|
| “Are you replacing pharmacists or doctors?” | No. Makoha is designed to support registered professionals and preserve their independent judgement, with doctor or urgent-care escalation beyond pharmacist scope. |
| “Is Ken the sole clinical authority?” | No. Ken is the clinician-founder and executive owner of the mission and product thesis. Release, protocols, evidence and incidents require appropriately qualified clinical, pharmacy, regulatory and quality authorities. |
| “Is this regulator approved?” | No approval claim is made. Makoha is planning on a regulated SaMD pathway, with provisional product classifications awaiting qualified confirmation. |
| “Has it been clinically validated?” | Not for patient use. Current evidence is controlled synthetic/mock and engineering assurance; formal clinical evidence remains gated. |
| “Is it an AI doctor?” | It is software supporting pharmacist and doctor workflows. It is not a registered practitioner and does not autonomously provide care. |
| “Will referrals be driven by fees?” | Clinical routing must be independent of revenue and audited. Doctor capacity may be funded through blended fees and service agreements, but referral criteria remain clinical. |

## Claim ladder

### Permitted now, subject to normal review

- “Clinician-founded Australian health-technology venture.”
- “In controlled development using synthetic data.”
- “Designed to support accountable pharmacist and doctor judgement.”
- “Building consultation-assurance and continuity infrastructure for pharmacist-led care.”
- “Planning for a regulated SaMD pathway and Class III-capable enterprise controls.”

### Conditional on specified evidence

- Founder qualifications, registration, years or specialty — verified biography pack.
- Named clinical or regulatory advisers — accepted appointment and permission to publish.
- “Clinically validated” — approved study and signed evidence conclusion.
- “Regulator approved” or ARTG language — actual inclusion/authorisation and approved wording.
- “Improves safety, time or outcomes” — appropriately designed and reviewed evidence.
- “Available doctor escalation” — executed provider agreement and monitored service levels.

### Prohibited now

- “AI diagnoses,” “AI prescribes,” “autonomous doctor” or equivalent.
- “Class III approved,” “TGA approved” or “ARTG listed.”
- “Patient-ready,” “production-ready” or “clinically proven.”
- Unverified founder credentials, partnerships, results or endorsements.
- Claims that Makoha owns patient data or guarantees safety/outcomes.

This posture reflects current Ahpra guidance that practitioners retain responsibility and must apply human judgement when using AI, and current TGA guidance that AI-enabled clinical decision support may be regulated as a medical device. Sources: [Ahpra AI guidance](https://www.ahpra.gov.au/Resources/Artificial-Intelligence-in-healthcare.aspx), [TGA software-based medical-device guidance](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/health-professional-information-and-resources/software-based-medical-devices-health-professionals).
