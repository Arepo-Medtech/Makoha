## Six-week scope

### Week 1 — Authority, goals and safety boundary

- Confirm group sponsor and discovery participants.
- Record Toowoomba ownership/counterparty as `TBC` if it cannot yet be resolved.
- Agree the non-clinical scope, confidentiality treatment and stop conditions.
- Freeze the current problem hypotheses and success/failure criteria.

### Weeks 2–3 — Workflow and system discovery

- Interview one group buyer/clinical-governance leader, one Toowoomba prescribing pharmacist and one technology/workflow owner.
- Map eligibility, history, exclusions, red flags, escalation, management, communication, documentation, audit and incident handling.
- Map existing clinical-system support and identify duplication risk.
- Do not collect patient-identifiable or patient-level information.

### Week 4 — Synthetic assurance walkthrough

- Configure synthetic cases representing agreed workflow points.
- Demonstrate Makoha's proposed documentation, exclusion, escalation and audit-assurance behaviour.
- Keep all outputs outside clinical care and clearly labelled non-clinical/synthetic.

### Week 5 — Risk, usability and operating-model tabletop

- Tabletop failure modes, override/stop authority, pharmacist accountability, workflow burden, integration and privacy/security boundaries.
- Record critical objections and required design changes.

### Week 6 — Evidence report and decision

- Deliver the agreed outputs.
- Apply the pass/revise/stop criteria.
- If warranted, draft a separately approved evidence-phase statement of work; no automatic continuation.

## Acceptance criteria

**PASS to consider a separate evidence phase:**

1. Pharmacy 777 identifies a material assurance problem not adequately solved by the current clinical system.
2. At least one authorised pharmacist user ranks it among their top three workflow or safety problems.
3. A synthetic walkthrough demonstrates a non-duplicative, usable role.
4. No critical safety, professional-accountability, privacy, security or regulatory objection remains unbounded.
5. An authorised sponsor provides a written next step.
6. Toowoomba execution parties and site authority are either confirmed or explicitly listed as pre-execution conditions.

**REVISE:** the problem is real but the user, workflow point, site, integration or offer structure is wrong.

**STOP:** value depends on autonomous clinical decision-making, patient-facing use before gates, unapproved data, exclusive rights, or a function the existing system already performs adequately.
