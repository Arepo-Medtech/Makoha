## Funding thesis

### Recommended structure

Pursue a **milestone-linked pre-seed capital plan**, sized from verified inputs rather than a legacy headline:

1. If verified runway is below the time needed to close counsel, QMS and authority foundations, arrange a tightly scoped bridge sufficient for the first control stage.
2. Use the Base enterprise evidence case as the principal 12-month capital envelope.
3. Treat grants, partner-funded evidence and other non-dilutive sources as offsets—not as dependencies for safety-critical work.
4. Do not rely on production revenue, patient fees or doctor-service margin before the required legal, clinical, regulatory and release gates.
5. Reforecast monthly and stop or narrow scope before cash pressure can weaken clinical or quality controls.

### Provisional capital implication

Before verified opening cash and liabilities, the Base case implies **[ASSUMPTION — LOW CONFIDENCE] $1,475,440** of financing capacity, including the 20% reserve and $50,000 of potential evidence revenue.

This does not approve a $1.475 million raise. It identifies the envelope that must be reconciled against actual cash, liabilities, committed funding, supplier quotes and founder compensation.

### Instrument and control principles

The funding instrument remains **TBC with corporate, legal and tax advice**. A SAFE/convertible instrument, priced equity or staged combination should be compared only after the current cap table, ESIC position, valuation evidence and target investor set are verified.

Regardless of instrument:

- preserve founder voting and operating control appropriate to stage;
- do not grant Pharmacy 777 or any strategic investor exclusivity;
- do not grant ownership of patient/customer data or Makoha’s background IP;
- avoid rights of first refusal over company sale, broad field-of-use restrictions, price parity or strategic vetoes that reduce future competition;
- reserve clinical and patient-safety decisions to the approved governance system, not investor consent;
- keep information, pro-rata and consent rights proportionate; and
- model dilution across the current register, any option pool and the next institutional round before signing.

### Financing alternatives

| Route | Use | Advantage | Risk/control response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder/insider bridge | Prevent an immediate control-stage cash gap | Fast and simple if capacity exists | Document terms and solvency; do not assume founder capacity |
| Pre-seed equity or convertible | Fund Base enterprise evidence plan | Matches regulated build and evidence milestones | Cap-table, dilution, valuation, tax and control review required |
| Grants/non-dilutive programmes | Offset evidence, R&D or commercialisation work | Extends runway without dilution | Do not make critical safety work dependent on uncertain awards |
| Partner-funded evidence | Recover defined evidence-phase costs | Creates commercial validation | Non-exclusive; separate data/IP rights; no automatic rollout |
| Strategic investment | Add distribution or sector capability | May accelerate pharmacy access | Reject exclusivity, data capture, field restriction or control leakage |
| Debt | Only against credible repayment capacity | Avoids immediate dilution | Not suitable as the default while revenue and runway are unverified |

This is strategic planning, not licensed financial, legal or tax advice.
