## 5. Decision

### Chosen wedge

**Makoha will enter as a governance-first consultation-assurance co-pilot for trained community pharmacist prescribers delivering defined expanded-scope services.**

The first product should combine:

- structured history and consultation capture;
- protocol- and evidence-linked prompts;
- fail-closed red-flag and out-of-scope escalation;
- pharmacist-reviewed management and prescribing support;
- audit-ready clinical documentation and decision provenance; and
- longitudinal record capture that compounds into (AU)PAIR.

The pharmacist remains the decision-maker and signer. Makoha does not prescribe autonomously, diagnose autonomously, or bypass a pharmacist's professional obligations.

### Positioning boundary

Makoha is not “another AI pharmacy reasoning engine.” Its beachhead is the **assurance layer for expanded-scope consultations**: prove that each consultation was sufficiently elicited, appropriately escalated, evidence-supported, professionally reviewed and durably recorded.

This boundary avoids three weaker positions:

- a generic AI scribe, which underuses the built safety/evidence asset;
- dispensing/script review, where direct competitors and incumbents are already entrenched; and
- a patient-first assistant, which activates the highest regulatory and acquisition burden before Makoha has clinical distribution.

### Launch boundary

The wedge decision does **not** authorise supply or live patient use. Until the regulatory route is documented, validation is limited to synthetic, retrospective or other counsel-approved evidence-generation modes. Any real-data activity also remains privacy-gated.

## 6. Strongest objection

The strongest objection is regulatory, not technical: the current TGA guidance appears to foreclose the inherited exempt-CDSS route for an AI-enabled reasoning product. Human sign-off, transparency and an image/signal firewall remain essential safety controls, but they do not by themselves restore exemption eligibility.

Therefore Makoha must evaluate a regulated-device/ARTG pathway, a genuinely non-device administrative product boundary, or another lawful evidence pathway with qualified counsel before product supply.

## 7. Strategic trade-offs

### What Makoha gains

- A buyer/user pair with an expanding, government-created workflow.
- Strong founder credibility and direct clinical relevance.
- Maximum reuse of the current safety, evidence, portal, audit and pharmacology build.
- A natural route to longitudinal data through repeated consultations.
- A narrow story for design partners and investors.

### What Makoha gives up

- Immediate direct-to-consumer reach.
- A superficially faster “AI scribe” launch.
- The assumption that an exempt-CDSS notification alone is sufficient.
- Broad multi-profession positioning during initial validation.

### What must stop

- Treating the inherited exempt-CDSS conclusion as probable or near-settled.
- Describing Makoha as ready for live pharmacist decision support.
- Expanding into patient-facing explanation, imaging or signal interpretation before the regulatory pathway is selected.
- Building generic pharmacy reasoning features that do not strengthen consultation assurance or longitudinal evidence.

## 8. Validation and kill criteria

The wedge remains active only while all of the following survive testing:

1. **Buyer pull:** after 12 structured interviews across pharmacists and pharmacy owners, at least five pharmacists identify consultation safety/documentation as a top-three problem and at least two owners agree to a written next step toward a pilot. Otherwise reposition or pause.
2. **Workflow value:** in a blinded, lawful evaluation, Makoha must improve documentation completeness or reduce documentation time without increasing critical omissions. Exact endpoints and sample size require the approved evidence protocol.
3. **Safety:** any critical under-triage attributable to the system, any bypass of human sign-off, or any unverifiable dose/recommendation is a stop condition until remediated and re-attested.
4. **Regulatory feasibility:** if qualified advice shows no affordable evidence/supply pathway within verified runway, pause commercial deployment and pivot to the non-clinical assurance/infrastructure lane while financing the regulated pathway.
5. **Data moat:** if pharmacy agreements prevent lawful longitudinal-data retention, patient control or defensible derived-learning rights, the wedge fails the moat test.
6. **Competitive differentiation:** if target buyers view Makoha as substitutable with existing pharmacy CDS or documentation tools after seeing the assurance workflow, narrow further or pivot to the licensable governance layer.
