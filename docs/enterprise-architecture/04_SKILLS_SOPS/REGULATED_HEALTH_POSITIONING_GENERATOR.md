# Regulated-Health Positioning and Claims Generator

**Skill ID:** S-M006  
**Status:** DRAFT — used once  
**Owner:** Founder / Growth / Clinical governance / Legal

## Trigger

Use when creating or revising founder biographies, company positioning, clinical product messaging, partner introductions, authority pages or claims for regulated healthcare.

## Inputs

- Controlled founder and entity facts.
- Current product intended purpose and user.
- Current engineering, clinical, regulatory and commercial status.
- Audience and use case.
- Named authorities and verified credentials.
- Claims evidence and approvals.
- Brand values and language constraints.

## Procedure

1. Separate verified facts, founder-supplied facts, assumptions and missing evidence.
2. State the strongest trust objection before choosing the narrative.
3. Separate founder leadership from clinical, pharmacy, quality, regulatory and release authority.
4. Write company, founder and category positioning in plain language.
5. Build a story only from evidenced events; preserve `[MISSING]` fields rather than reconstructing them.
6. Define three to six messaging pillars with present proof and claim boundaries.
7. Create audience-specific variants and objection responses.
8. Build a claim ladder: permitted now, conditional on evidence and prohibited.
9. Cross-check product state, decision log, gap register and current regulator guidance.
10. Record the positioning decision, authority gaps, artefacts and next gate.

## Outputs

- Master positioning statement.
- Evidence-safe founder narrative.
- Short and long biographies.
- Messaging pillars and audience variants.
- Claim ladder and language rules.
- Clinical-authority blueprint or link to the current one.
- Decision/gap/register updates.

## Guardrails

- Never invent qualifications, registration, experience, outcomes, partners or endorsements.
- “Dr” or “clinician” does not establish profession, registration status, specialty or authority scope.
- Never describe software as a registered practitioner or autonomous clinical decision-maker.
- Never claim approval, validation, safety, performance or availability beyond current evidence.
- Founder authority cannot replace profession-specific and independent review.
- Clinical routing and public claims remain independent of revenue and growth pressure.
- Legacy narrative is evidence to verify, not copy to republish.

## Maturity rule

Promote from `DRAFT` only after independent legal/clinical review and successful use for a second audience or materially different regulated product.
