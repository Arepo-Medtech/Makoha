# S-M003 — Two-Sided Regulated-Health ICP Builder

**Status:** DRAFT  
**Trigger:** selecting or revising an entry customer for a regulated health product where buyer, professional user and patient are different people

## Purpose

Prevent a generic persona from hiding purchasing authority, professional accountability or patient consequence.

## Procedure

1. Freeze the proposed product boundary and intended user.
2. Name the economic buyer, daily professional user, patient beneficiary and material influencers separately.
3. State one situation-based job-to-be-done for each primary role.
4. Identify current workflow, mandated systems, workarounds and competing substitutes.
5. Define qualification signals and explicit anti-ICP conditions.
6. Record buying triggers, decision path, budget path and adoption objections.
7. Use real customer language only when sourced and permissioned; distinguish public programme language from customer testimony.
8. Convert every low-confidence belief into an interview or workflow-evidence test.
9. Set pass, revise and kill thresholds before interviews.
10. Update the decision and gap registers; never describe the ICP as validated until thresholds are met.

## Minimum output

- Segment-priority table.
- Buyer/user/patient role map.
- Jobs-to-be-done and objections.
- Existing-system duplication test.
- Twelve-interview evidence plan or an equivalent justified sample.
- Explicit field-validation status.

## Safety rules

- Never recruit patients into clinical product use through a discovery interview.
- Do not collect patient-identifiable information in general research notes.
- Do not imply approval, efficacy or clinical readiness.
- Treat requests for autonomous clinical decisions as disqualifying unless a separately authorised regulated pathway exists.

## Review rule

Promote from DRAFT only after a second market/segment application and independent review by commercial and clinical-governance owners.
