# S-M004 — Regulated-Health Design-Partner Offer Builder

**Status:** DRAFT  
**Trigger:** packaging a first partner offer before clinical deployment or full commercial validation

## Purpose

Create a credible low-risk offer that generates decision-grade evidence without implying approval, efficacy or clinical availability.

## Procedure

1. Freeze the intended user, problem and prohibited uses.
2. Separate discovery, synthetic evaluation, shadow evidence and clinical deployment.
3. Name the buyer, professional user, site owner, clinical-accountability owner and data parties; label unknowns `TBC`.
4. Define a short fixed scope with named participants and deliverables.
5. State price, internal/third-party costs, term, termination and non-conversion terms.
6. Define non-exclusivity across territory, segment, function, condition, channel and time.
7. Allocate background IP, generalisable learning, confidential information, data, model-training and publication rights.
8. Set pass, revise and stop thresholds before the work begins.
9. Make regulatory, privacy, security, site-authority and clinical-use gates explicit.
10. Produce both a controlled internal offer and a plain-language one-page counterpart document.

## Safety rules

- Never use discovery permission as clinical-use permission.
- Never request patient data when synthetic evidence can answer the question.
- Never treat a group signature as site authority without evidence.
- Never exchange free work for exclusivity, uncontrolled data rights or unsupported public endorsement.
- Do not promise clinical, time, revenue or safety outcomes without evidence.

## Minimum acceptance

- Scope and exclusions.
- Price and term.
- Partner and Makoha contributions.
- IP/data/publication rights.
- Pass/revise/stop thresholds.
- Execution gates and owner.

## Review rule

Promote from DRAFT only after a second use and independent commercial, legal and clinical-governance review.
