# Regulated-Health Business-Model Stress Test

**Skill ID:** S-M005  
**Status:** DRAFT — used once  
**Owner:** Strategy / Finance / Clinical governance

## Trigger

Use when choosing or revising who pays, the billing unit, a regulated-health revenue model or a claimed data moat.

## Inputs

- Frozen entry wedge and intended users.
- Economic buyer, clinical user and patient beneficiary.
- Regulatory/product boundaries and supply gates.
- Existing-system and named-competitor evidence.
- Current delivery, quality, clinical, integration and support costs.
- Proposed data rights and evidence protocol.

## Procedure

1. Separate payer, buyer, user, beneficiary, controller and accountable clinician.
2. Model at least three architectures: per-user, per-event and enterprise/platform.
3. Test each for incentive safety, procurement fit, revenue predictability, support burden, regulatory cost recovery and scalability.
4. Define recurring, implementation and evidence-phase revenue separately.
5. Produce low, working and high sensitivities; label every unsupported value as an assumption.
6. Calculate ARR and gross profit, then list every missing input that prevents credible CAC, LTV, payback or break-even claims.
7. Stress-test against direct product, substitute workflow and vertically integrated competitors.
8. State the moat as a legally obtainable asset stack; identify data ownership, permission, provenance, portability, deletion and training limits.
9. Set falsifiers and kill criteria before external pricing.
10. Record the decision, gaps, validation owner and next evidence gate.

## Required outputs

- Selected architecture and rejected alternatives.
- Business Model Canvas.
- Unit-economic sensitivity table.
- Moat memo and data-rights boundaries.
- Validation plan, falsifiers and kill criteria.
- Decision and gap-register updates.

## Guardrails

- Do not represent assumptions as forecasts or approved prices.
- A per-consult fee may be used only with transparent financial consent, access safeguards and audited separation between clinical routing and revenue; never reward unnecessary activity, referral or prescribing.
- Do not claim ownership of patient data or a network effect without explicit rights and measured improvement.
- Do not infer group, site, clinician, patient or ethics authority from another party's consent.
- Do not price a documentation feature as a regulated assurance product without evidence of incremental value.
- Do not issue a paid offer until direct costs and approval authority are verified.

## Maturity rule

Promote from `DRAFT` only after independent review and a second materially different commercial decision reproduces useful results.
