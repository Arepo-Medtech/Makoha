# S-M001 — Wedge/Opportunity Scorer

**Status:** DRAFT  
**Purpose:** Select one entry wedge for a regulated health-technology venture without ignoring founder constraints, current build leverage or regulatory reality.

## Triggers

- “Choose the first wedge.”
- “Reassess our market entry.”
- “Which segment/use case should we pursue first?”

## Required inputs

- Candidate wedges.
- Founder skills, credibility, network, capacity and risk tolerance.
- Current product/build state.
- Buyer/user/beneficiary model.
- Current market and competitor evidence.
- Current regulatory constraints.
- Budget, runway and desired evidence window.

Missing inputs are marked `[MISSING: field]`; no figures or commitments are invented.

## Procedure

1. Use toolkit prompt #001 as the base.
2. Define three to five mutually exclusive wedges.
3. Score market pull, founder fit, build leverage, time to evidence, regulatory tractability, moat and capital efficiency.
4. State the strongest objection to the leading option.
5. Select one primary wedge and one fallback; pause the rest.
6. Define positioning boundaries, stop-doing decisions and measurable kill criteria.
7. Record the decision, gaps, evidence and next gate.

## Guardrails

- No patient-facing recommendation may outrun clinical/regulatory gates.
- Human accountability must remain explicit.
- Regulatory classifications require current primary sources and qualified advice.
- Protect founder equity, IP and longitudinal-data rights.
- A high score cannot override a critical safety or legality blocker.

## Output contract

- Evidence summary.
- Weighted decision matrix.
- One selected wedge and one fallback.
- Strongest objection.
- Trade-offs and stop-doing list.
- Validation plan and kill criteria.
- Register updates and one next action.

## Attestation path

Promote to `ACTIVE` after independent review. Promote to `ATTESTED` after a second real wedge decision demonstrates that the scoring and kill criteria produce a clear, auditable result.
